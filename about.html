Our Identity

A Cooperative Ministry for the Global Gospel.

Uganda Baptist Seminary (UBS) represents a strategic partnership between the Baptist Mission of Uganda (IMB), the Baptist Union of Uganda, and World Venture International.

By employing a decentralized, modular approach, we empower students to remain embedded in their local context. Our intensive four-week sessions, held three times annually, bridge the gap between rigorous academic theology and authentic field ministry.

Our Vision

"We are servants of the church leaders of East Africa... to make the word of God fully known, warning and teaching everyone with all wisdom, so that we may present everyone mature in Christ."

Our Goal

To equip the next generation of East African church leaders with sound theological education, ensuring they become excellent servants who reflect God's glory in their communities.

Historical Narrative

A Legacy of Faithfulness

Established 1988
1988

The Foundation

Rev. Jack Frost pioneered the seminary in Jinja, launching the inaugural class within the classrooms of Nile Baptist Church.

1989

Strategic Acquisition

UBS secured 2.6 acres of land on Rubaga Hill, establishing a permanent home for Baptist theological training overlooking Jinja.

2010

NCHE Accreditation

A milestone for academic excellence: UBS achieved full accreditation from the National Council of Higher Education (NCHE) for Diploma-level studies.

Today

A Regional Hub for East Africa

Serving nearly 500 students across 8 nations—including Kenya, Tanzania, South Sudan, Rwanda, Burundi, and DRC.
